Custom Home Design Review Process

Welcome to Your Design Journey

Building your custom luxury home in Canyon Pass is an exciting journey that begins with understanding our thoughtful design review process. As a distinguished homeowner in this exceptional Dove Mountain community, you'll work directly with the Canyon Pass Architectural Review Committee to ensure your home reflects both your vision and our community's commitment to environmental harmony and architectural excellence.

What You Need to Know

  • The Dove Mountain DRC manages conservation at the master-plan level and works with developers, not individual homeowners
  • You submit your home plans to the Canyon Pass Architectural Review Committee (ARC)
  • You do not pay separate DRC fees—those are handled at the developer level
  • Your ARC review covers home placement, architecture, materials, landscaping, and site design
  • A $10,000 Construction Compliance Deposit is required and will be refunded upon successful project completion

Step One

Pre-Design Check

Your Responsibilities

Confirm your specific lot number, tract designation, and recorded building envelope boundaries
Review any height restrictions or elevation caps that apply to your particular lot
Familiarize yourself with the Canyon Pass CC&Rs, Tract Declarations, and Design Guidelines
Schedule a consultation with the ARC management or architectural consultant to discuss your vision and any questions

What the ARC Provides

Access to all governing documents, design guidelines, and your lot-specific requirements
Guidance on building envelope specifications and any unique restrictions for your property
Professional consultation to help you understand the review process and requirements
Pro Tip: This initial phase is crucial for a smooth approval process. Understanding your lot's unique characteristics and restrictions now will save time and prevent costly revisions later.
Step Two

Preliminary Design Submittal

Required Documentation

Comprehensive site plan showing your home's footprint positioned within the approved building envelope
Preliminary floor plans illustrating the layout and spatial flow of your home
Exterior elevations from all sides, showing architectural character and massing
Basic grading plan and proposed driveway layout
Preliminary designs for any major walls, courtyards, gates, or ancillary structures

ARC Review Process

The Architectural Review Committee will carefully evaluate your preliminary submission to ensure alignment with:

Canyon Pass CC&Rs and applicable Tract Declarations
Height limitations and elevation caps specific to your lot
Community Design Guidelines and architectural standards
Site development requirements and environmental considerations
What to Expect: The ARC will either approve your preliminary design to move forward to final submittal, or provide specific feedback and request revisions to bring your plans into compliance with community standards.
Step Three

Final Design Submittal

Complete Documentation Package

Full architectural plans with detailed elevations from all perspectives
Comprehensive grading and drainage plan ensuring proper water management
Exterior materials and color board showcasing all finishes, textures, and palette selections
Complete landscape plan including plant species list (emphasizing native and desert-adapted plants), irrigation design, and hardscape elements
Exterior lighting plan showing fixture locations, styles, and specifications
Any retaining walls, outdoor living spaces, pools, or water features

ARC Final Review

Upon receiving your complete final submittal, the ARC will conduct a thorough review and:

Issue formal approval with any specific conditions or implementation notes
Identify any required professional survey work for lots with height control restrictions
Provide written documentation of all approved specifications and materials
Outline the next steps for pre-construction requirements
Important: Final approval is contingent upon your plans meeting all community standards. Any subsequent changes to approved plans that affect exterior appearance, grading, or height will require resubmittal for review.
Step Four

Pre-Construction Requirements

Financial Requirements

Construction Compliance Deposit

$10,000

Purpose: Ensures compliance with approved plans and protects common areas during construction

Held In: FDIC-insured interest-bearing account managed by the Association

Interest: Accrued interest belongs to the Association and supports Common Area maintenance

Refund: Full deposit returned upon successful project completion, minus any costs for compliance corrections or damage repairs

Pay applicable ARC review fees according to the current fee schedule
Submit Construction Compliance Deposit of $10,000

Documentation Requirements

Provide complete contractor information and certificates of insurance as required
Submit construction timeline and project schedule
For height-controlled lots: Arrange for a licensed surveyor to confirm that planned elevations comply with maximum height restrictions
Acknowledge receipt of all ARC conditions and requirements

Authorization to Proceed

Once all fees, deposits, and required documentation are confirmed, the ARC will issue written authorization to begin construction under the approved plans. Construction may not commence without this formal authorization.

Critical Reminder: No construction activities may begin until you receive written authorization from the ARC. Beginning work without approval may result in stop-work orders and potential deposit forfeiture.
Step Five

Construction & Inspections

Your Construction Responsibilities

Build strictly in accordance with all ARC-approved plans and specifications
Submit any design changes affecting exterior appearance, site grading, or building height for ARC review and approval before implementation
Protect neighboring properties, common areas, and natural washes from construction damage or debris
Maintain a clean and organized construction site that reflects the character of Canyon Pass
Ensure all contractors and subcontractors understand and comply with community standards

ARC Oversight & Inspections

During the construction phase, the ARC and Association may:

Conduct periodic site visits to verify compliance with approved plans
Review construction progress and address any concerns promptly
For height-controlled lots: Require interim or final elevation surveys to certify compliance with maximum height restrictions
Document any deviations from approved plans and request immediate correction
Modification Protocol: Any changes to approved plans—no matter how minor—that affect the exterior appearance, grading, drainage, or structural height must be submitted to the ARC for written approval before proceeding. Unapproved changes may result in required removal or modification at your expense.

Communication During Construction

Maintain open communication with the ARC throughout construction. Early notification of any potential issues or needed modifications will facilitate smoother resolution and help ensure your project stays on schedule.

Step Six

Project Closeout & Deposit Refund

Final Inspection Process

Request a final inspection from the ARC upon substantial completion of your home
For height-controlled lots: Provide final elevation survey from a licensed surveyor certifying compliance with maximum height limits
Ensure all landscaping, irrigation, and exterior lighting are installed per approved plans
Confirm that common areas, neighboring properties, and natural features are undamaged and restored if disturbed
Remove all construction debris, equipment, and temporary structures from the site

ARC Final Verification

The Architectural Review Committee will conduct a comprehensive final inspection to verify:

Construction matches all approved architectural plans, elevations, and specifications
Exterior materials, colors, and finishes are as approved
Landscaping and hardscaping comply with approved designs
No damage to common areas, shared infrastructure, or neighboring properties
Site cleanup is complete and the property presents in accordance with community standards

Construction Compliance Deposit Resolution

Deposit Refund Process

$10,000

Full Compliance: If your project fully complies with all approved plans and no repairs to common areas are needed, your entire $10,000 deposit will be refunded.

Corrections Required: If the Association incurred costs to correct violations or repair damage to common areas, those documented expenses will be deducted from your deposit, with the balance refunded to you.

Interest: All interest earned on your deposit during the construction period is retained by the Association and allocated to Common Area maintenance and improvements.

Congratulations! Upon successful completion and final approval, you'll have created a custom luxury home that enhances the distinctive character of Canyon Pass while reflecting your personal vision. Welcome home to Dove Mountain.
